## 2. App Purpose, Problem Solved, and Value

**Purpose:** Confirmation Saints is a bilingual (English/Spanish) reference app that helps Catholic Confirmation candidates research, compare, and choose a patron saint for the Sacrament of Confirmation.

**Problem it solves:** Choosing a Confirmation saint is a meaningful but often difficult part of the Confirmation preparation process. Candidates — typically teenagers, but also adults receiving the sacrament later in life — often have only a few weeks to research dozens of saints, understand their patronages, and pick one whose life resonates with their own interests, background, or faith journey. Printed resources are limited, parish materials vary widely, and general web searches surface inconsistent or unreliable information. Candidates and their sponsors need a focused, trustworthy, and easy-to-browse tool.

**Value to the intended audience:**
- Over 70 Catholic saints with curated biographies, feast days, patronages, inspirational quotes, and source citations from trusted Catholic publishers (Loyola Press, FOCUS, Lifeteen, Ascension Press, Hallow, and the Catholic Encyclopedia).
- Rich filtering to help candidates find saints by interest (sports, music, science, education, medicine), region (Europe, Americas, Africa, Asia, Middle East), historical era, age group (young saints), life state (married, religious, martyrs), and gender.
- Full bilingual experience — all saint biographies, categories, and UI strings are available in English and Spanish and switch instantly.
- Educational "What is Confirmation?" section explaining the sacrament and the tradition of choosing a Confirmation name.
- Completely offline — no internet, no account, no tracking, no ads. Ideal for teens who may use shared or school-managed devices.

**Intended audience:** Catholic teenagers preparing for Confirmation (primary audience), adult Confirmation candidates, Confirmation sponsors, catechists, and parents assisting candidates.

## 3. Accessing and Reviewing the App's Main Features

**No login credentials are required.** The app has no accounts, no sign-in, no gated content, and no server backend. All features are available immediately on first launch.

**Suggested review path:**

1. **Launch the app.** A one-time welcome screen introduces the app. Tap "Get Started."
2. **Saints tab (bottom nav).** Browse the full list of 70+ saints. Scroll or use the search bar to find saints by name. Tap any saint to view their full profile — biography, feast day, patronages, a quote, and source links.
3. **Explore tab.** Browse saints by category: Interests, Regions, Eras, Age, Life State, Gender. Tap a category, then a sub-category (e.g., Interests → Music) to see matching saints.
4. **About tab.** Read the educational content explaining what the Sacrament of Confirmation is and the tradition of choosing a patron saint.
5. **Settings tab.** Change the app language between English and Spanish using the language picker at the top. All saint content and UI update immediately. The Settings tab also contains links to the Privacy Policy, Support page, and app version info.

## 4. External Services, Tools, and Platforms

**Confirmation Saints uses no external services for its core functionality.** The app is fully self-contained:

- All saint content (biographies, images, categories) is bundled inside the app at build time.
- All data loads from local JSON files packaged with the app.
- The app makes **no network requests** during normal operation.
- There is no backend server, no API, no authentication service, no analytics SDK, no ads SDK, no payment processor, no AI/ML service, and no third-party tracking.

**Content attribution (sources used during content creation, not runtime):** Biographical and factual information about saints was researched and summarized from publicly available Catholic reference materials, including Loyola Press, FOCUS, Lifeteen, Ascension Press, Hallow, and the Catholic Encyclopedia. These are acknowledged in the app's About/Credits section and in individual saint source citations. These publishers are not integrated as services — they are cited references only.

**Images:** Saint images are sourced from Wikimedia Commons (public domain and Creative Commons licensed works) and are bundled with the app.

**The only external links in the app are:**
- Links to the Privacy Policy (static GitHub Pages HTML page)
- Links to the Support page (static GitHub Pages HTML page with a contact form)
- Optional "Learn more" links on individual saint detail pages that point to the cited source URLs (opened in Safari/the system browser)
